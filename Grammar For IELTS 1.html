1. Present Simple


- Regular habits or repeated actions:



- Permanent situations



- Facts or generally accepted truth



- Instructions or directions



- Stories, films, books and plays

Regular habits or repeated actions:

- Examples:

  • I get up really early and practice for an hour or so most days.
  • I use the Internet just about every day.
  • I wake up reluctantly at six in the morning.
  • I drag my feet to the bathroom and wash my face. I don’t take my shower in the morning. I prefer taking it in the evening after a hard day at work.
  • I get dressed in my walk-in wardrobe. I always put on a suit and a neck tie because I work at the bank.
  • I have breakfast with my wife then I brush my teeth.
  • Afterwards, I drive to my workplace which is about thirty kilometres away from my house. I arrive there around eight o’clock.
  • I work until half past twelve.
  • I have lunch with my colleagues. We eat well-prepared meals at the restaurant.
  • We often feel sleepy in the afternoon but we make sure that our deadlines are met.
  • I leave the office at five. I stop by the supermarket to get a few foodstuffs.
  • We prepare dinner together while talking about our day.
  • We have our evening meal at eight o’clock.
  • I take my shower after dinner.
  • We go to bed at 10:30 p.m.

- Always, Generally, Normally, Usually, Often, Sometimes, Rarely, Never, Every day, Every evening

(Mostly) Permanent situations:


- Examples:

  • My parents own a restaurant.
  • She doesn’t speak English.
  • ... plays football.
  • She teaches German.
  • We live in California.
  • I play tennis and basketball.
  • My father works at an elementary school.

Facts or generally accepted truth:


- Examples:

  • Students don't generally have much money.
  • If you heat water to 100 C, It boils.
  • A magnet attracts iron.
  • The sun rises in the east and sets in the west.
  • Smoking causes cancer.
  • Five times five equals twenty-five.
  • Babies eat, sleep and cry.
  • Flowers need sunlight and water to grow.

- Generally, mainly, Normally, Usually, Traditionally

Instructions or directions:


- Examples:

  • You go down to the traffic lights, then you turn left.
  • To start the programme, first you click on the icon on the desktop.
  • Open the door.
  • Leave me alone.
  • Submit the report tomorrow.
  • Be here at 5.
  • Don't leave me alone.
  • Never give up.
  • Always remeber that every single minute in your life matters.
  • Boil the milk and then add it to the mixture.

Stories, films, books and plays:


- Examples:

  • I really enjoyed reading Aldous Huxley’s ‘Brave New World’ and one scene in particular comes to mind. Two of the characters, Linda and her son John, are taken from the place where they are living, to London. This is the ‘brave new world’ and ideals of love, and family life are despised. Because Linda looks old and unattractive she is given a drug called ‘soma’ in the hope that she will die. Eventually, John gets a phone call and is told his mum is dying in hospital. He rushes to her bedside and sits there watching over her. He remembers how she used to be when she was younger but a group of children spoil the moment when they start making fun of Linda. This event is really important because it shows the difference between the old and the new world. Even the nurse can’t understand why John is unhappy when his mum dies.

2. Present Continuous


- Temporary situations



- Actions happening now



- Trends or changing situations



- Things that happen more often than expected


Temporary situations:


- Examples:

  • I'm studying really hard for my exams.
  • My cousin is living in Thailand at the moment.
  • John’s driving his father’s car while his own car is in the workshop.
  • They’re temporarily living with his Uncle Fred. They’ll stay there until they find an apartment.
  • Julia’s working in Chicago this month (but she will be back in her office in LA next month).
  • I’m studying in Berlin this year (but I will return to my language studies in Cambridge at the end of the year).
  • Aisha’s currently improving her English, but she hopes to be able to enter the university in the USA soon.

- At the moment, Currently, Now, This week/month/year

Actions happening now:


- Examples:

  • I'm waiting for my friends.
  • Sorry, she can't come to the phone right now; she is having a bath.
  • Look! Someone is trying to break into your car.
  • Where's John? - He's playing soccer in the sports hall.
  • This calculator isn't working properly. Do you have another one.
  • You can go outside now. It isn't raining any more.
  • What are you doing? - My watch is broken and I'm trying to fix it.
  • Why are you talking? You should be listening to me.

Trends or changing situations:


- Examples:

  • The Internet is making it easier for people to stay in touch with each other.
  • The price of petrol is rising dramatically.
  • At the moment we are spending a lot on marketing, but brand recognition is not increasing fast enough.
  • The current economic outlook is good. The employment rate is going down and the salaries are raising.
  • People are living longer and longer thanks to medical advances.
  • Fewer and fewer people are buying newspapers. The Internet is becoming the primary source of information.
  • More and more forests are disappearing because of human activity.
  • The sea levels are rising.
  • The standard of living in poor areas is improving, but it is a slow process.

Things that happen more often than expected:


- Examples:

  • My mom's always saying I don't help enough. (complaint)
  • He's always visiting exciting places. (envy)
  • I’m seeing Jane a lot these days.
  • You're smoking too much
  • He's eating a lot these days.
  • She's swimming every morning
  • Lucy's always smiling!
  • She's constantly missing the train.
  • You're forever losing your keys!

3. State Verbs


- Thoughts



- Feelings



- Senses



- Possession



- Description

Thoughts:


  • Agree: Teenagers and their parents rarely agree.
  • Assume: I think we can safely assume that interest rates will go up again soon.
  • Believe: I don’t believe a word of it
  • Disagree: Barr strongly disagreed with Kronfeld’s statement.
  • Forget: He’s someone who never forgets a face.
  • Hope: Liam decided to ignore the warning and just hope for the best.
  • Know: If you must know, I was with James last night.
  • Regret: Don’t do anything you might regret.
  • Remember: They had three children, if I remember rightly.
  • Suppose: I suppose you’re right.
  • Think: Do you honestly think I would do something so stupid?
  • Understand: I don’t really understand the political situation in Egypt.

Feelings:


  • Adore: Betty adores her grandchildren.
  • Despise: She despised her neighbours.
  • Dislike: I dislike being the centre of attention.
  • Enjoy: I enjoyed every minute of it.
  • Feel: Do you still feel hungry?
  • Hate: It’s the kind of movie you either love or hate.
  • Like: How do you like living in London (=how much do you like it)?
  • Love: I love you, Tracy.
  • Mind: I don’t mind the heat, in fact I quite like it.
  • Prefer: I prefer to wear clothes made of natural fibers.
  • Want: They desperately want a son.

Senses:


  • Feel: She felt his forehead. Perhaps he had a temperature.
  • Hear: I couldn’t hear what they were saying most of the time.
  • See: You can see the Houses of Parliament from here.
  • Smell: The stew smells delicious.
  • Taste: This yoghurt tastes of strawberries.

Possession:


  • Have: She has dark hair and brown eyes.
  • Own: You need to get permission from the farmer who owns the land.
  • Belong: Put the chair back where it belongs.

Description:


  • Appear: It appears that all the files have been deleted.
  • Contain: The museum contains a number of original artworks.
  • Look: It looks like they won’t be needing us anymore.
  • Look like: He looks like his grandfather.
  • Mean: What does ‘patronizing’ mean?
  • Resemble: It’s amazing how closely Brian and Steve resemble each other.
  • Seem: Ann doesn't seem very pleased.
  • Smell: It smells good.
  • Sound: Istanbul sounds really exciting.
  • Taste: The food tastes reaaly delicious.
  • Weigh: She weighs 50 kilos.
